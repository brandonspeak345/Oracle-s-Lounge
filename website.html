Celestial Journal

The Oracle stirred beneath velvet twilight, her fingers trailing constellations carved from memory and myth. Tonight, the stars murmured with dusty intent—cryptic syllables folded into the fabric of old wine and forgotten parchment. The Lounge exhaled incense and quiet jazz, its corners wrapped in timeworn silence. Patrons sipped concoctions named after eclipses, each one brewed to unearth the sorrow or spark buried in their souls. I wrote this entry with hands stained rouge, the ink sluggish and sweet. The cosmos doesn’t roar—it rustles. It hints. It leaves fragments like cocktail rings on antique bar tops, waiting to be decoded. Outside the stained-glass porthole, a comet traced a lingering question across the void.

The Oracle’s gaze lingered on the obsidian bottle etched with lunar glyphs. With each pour, gravity bent—not just the glass, but intention. I tasted prophecy laced in fig and absinthe, velvet and ash. Outside, constellations flickered like candle flames, uncertain and expectant. Inside, the Lounge held its breath, as if listening for the moment a soul confesses itself between sips. The journal grew heavier tonight. Not with weight, but with suggestion—pages pulling toward something unsaid. I scribbled, not to remember, but to keep the silence company. The music, slow and sepia-toned, curled around my thoughts like smoke. Even the glass fogged with memory, refracting the room into secrets.
